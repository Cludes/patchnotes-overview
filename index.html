Patch 12.1 “Curse of Ula’tek” Class Changes Full breakdown of the first 12.1 PTR build, with the math worked out for every number.

Theme: DPS cooldowns nerfed, steady-state buffed Captured: 2026-06-20 Source: Blizzard notes via Wowhead
Read this first. This is a first PTR build and values will change before live. Percentages below are computed directly from Blizzard’s stated old → new values using (new − old) / old, so they are exact for each line. The “biggest buffs/nerfs” and reroll sections are an interpretation of the direction and size of those changes - not simulation results. Blizzard’s stated intent for most DPS specs is roughly net-neutral total damage with a flatter profile, so a big cooldown nerf does not always mean a real DPS loss.

How the math works

Every numeric line is transcribed from the official notes and stored with its old and new value (or, where the notes only give a relative figure, that exact figure). The page then computes:

  • Change to the stated value = (new − old) / old × 100. Example: a shield that grants 40% → 50% of damage is +25% stronger, not “+10%”.
  • Direction (buff / nerf / neutral) is judged per line by gameplay impact. A shorter cooldown or lower mana cost is a buff even though the number goes down.
  • We deliberately do not invent net-DPS figures, because real throughput depends on uptime, scaling and final tuning that the notes do not provide. Coefficient changes are exact; whole-spec impact is described in words.

The dataset (data.js) and a verification script (verify.mjs) ship in the repo. Running node verify.mjs recomputes every percentage and checks integrity.

    Biggest buffs & biggest nerfs

    Grounded in the direction and size of the actual changes, separating genuine net gains/losses from the net-neutral redistributions above. Not a sim; a reading of the notes.

    Trending up (real net gains)

    1. Tanks, across the board. All six tank specs got defensives scaled to the +25% health change. Prot Paladin gained the most damage (Judgment/Consecration +100%, Shield of the Righteous +150%) plus Improved Ardent Defender (+20% max HP); Prot Warrior got Ignore Pain +25%, Ravager +50% and Demolish 45s→30s. Tanks are the clearest winners of the patch.
    2. Beast Mastery Hunter. The one DPS Blizzard outright says “felt weak” and buffed: Kill Command +20%, Cobra Shot +20%, Barbed Shot +25%, pet damage +100% (Razor Sharp), Beast Cleave 8s→10s. Only the AoE cleave was trimmed, so single-target is a real net gain.
    3. Resto Druid. +6% all healing, Verdancy +40%, Flash of Clarity (+40% Regrowth), Swiftmend bonus, and cheaper Rejuv/Regrowth/Lifebloom. Throughput and efficiency up.
    4. Elemental Shaman AoE. Single-target is a redistribution, but Chain Lightning +60% and now 5 targets (Farseer), plus Flame Shock +60%, are a big multi-target gain.
    5. Arms Warrior & Discipline Priest (minor). Arms: Ravager +50%, Demolish more frequent. Disc: Smite +40%, Flash Heal +25% with low offsetting loss.

    Trending down (real losses & squish risk)

    1. Mistweaver Monk. The only spec with a blanket −3% all healing, plus Spinning Crane Kick −15% and Way of the Crane 340%→280%. Mastery +50% softens it only if you stack Mastery. Net down.
    2. Burst-identity specs (squish risk). Net-neutral by design, but if the compensation is under-tuned these come out behind: Elemental (Ascendance Overload −60%), Windwalker (burst −25/30%), Devourer (meta scaling −66%), Fire (Combustion halved), Ret (Art of War −47%, Divine Toll −50%).
    3. Fatebound Rogues. Deal Fate extra-combo-point chance 100%→60% (−40%) hits both Assa/Outlaw Fatebound builds.
    4. Dark Ranger Hunters. Black Arrow direct damage −40% and Withering Fire’s Black Arrow −40%.
    5. Frost Mage Hero-talent burst. Hand of Frost R2 halved (1/2% → 0.5/1%).
    Untouched this build: Affliction & Demonology Warlock got no changes. Frost DK and Enhancement Shaman had no offensive changes (only a defensive/healing bump). If you main these, nothing in this build moves your damage.

    Should you reroll? (if these go live as-is)

    Short version: this is a redistribution patch on a first PTR build, so there is no “free DPS” reroll hiding in these notes, and the numbers will change. Don’t reroll off one PTR build. With that said, by direction of travel:

    Want to come out ahead no matter the final tuning? Roll a tank. Every tank got safer, and Prot Paladin / Prot Warrior also gained a lot of baseline damage and smoother threat. Lowest-risk “winner” reroll.
    Want a DPS that genuinely got stronger, not just reshuffled? Beast Mastery Hunter is the cleanest pick - buffed because it was weak, with no nerf to its own burst. Solid ranged, low complexity.
    Healer main? Resto Druid is the trend-up healer (throughput + efficiency). Disc/Holy/Pres are roughly neutral with quality-of-life wins. Avoid rerolling to Mistweaver purely for output this build.
    Hate spiky, feast-or-famine gameplay? That is the entire point of this patch. The smoothed specs - Elemental, Windwalker, Devourer, Fire, Retribution - will feel much flatter for similar totals. Reroll for feel, not numbers.
    Chasing big burst windows? Don’t reroll for this now. The on-use spikes are exactly what is being cut across the board - it is the worst build to pick a spec for its cooldown ceiling.
    AoE / Mythic+ focused? Elemental Shaman (Chain Lightning +60% + 5 targets, Flame Shock +60%) and Shadow Priest (new Shadeburst AoE) picked up dedicated multi-target damage.
    Reroll caveat: none of this is simmed. Net DPS depends on final coefficients, tier sets and encounter tuning that this build does not lock in. Treat it as “which way is the wind blowing,” not a tier list.
