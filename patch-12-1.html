World of Warcraft: Midnight · PTR Development Notes

Patch 12.1 “Curse of Ula’tek” Every class change across the 12.1 PTR builds (through the Week 3 weekly notes), with the math worked out for every number, a cooldown-squish scorecard, and a reroll read.

Read this first. This covers PTR builds 1-3 (through the July 8 weekly notes) and values will change. Later-build additions carry a Wk2 / Wk3 tag. Percentages are computed directly from Blizzard’s stated old → new values using (new - old) / old, so each line is exact. The “biggest buffs/nerfs” and reroll sections are an interpretation of the direction and size of those changes, not sims. Blizzard’s stated intent for most DPS specs is roughly net-neutral total damage with a flatter profile, so a big cooldown nerf does not always mean a real DPS loss.

    Biggest buffs & biggest nerfs

    Grounded in the direction and size of the actual changes, separating genuine net gains/losses from the net-neutral redistributions above. A reading of the notes, not a sim.

    Trending up

    1. Tanks, across the board. All six tank specs got defensives scaled to the +25% health change. Prot Paladin gained the most damage (Judgment/Consecration +100%, Shield of the Righteous +150%) plus +20% max HP on Ardent Defender; Prot Warrior got Ignore Pain +25%, Ravager +50%, Demolish 45s→30s. Clearest winners of the patch.
    2. Beast Mastery Hunter. The one DPS Blizzard says “felt weak” and buffed: Kill Command +20%, Cobra Shot +20%, Barbed Shot +25%, pet damage +100% (Razor Sharp), Beast Cleave 8s→10s. Only AoE cleave trimmed, so single-target is a real gain.
    3. Resto Druid. +6% all healing, Verdancy +40%, Flash of Clarity (+40% Regrowth), Swiftmend bonus, cheaper Rejuv/Regrowth/Lifebloom. Throughput and efficiency up.
    4. Elemental Shaman AoE. Single-target is a redistribution, but Chain Lightning +60% at 5 targets (Farseer) plus Flame Shock +60% is a big multi-target gain.
    5. Arms Warrior & Discipline Priest (minor). Arms: Ravager +50%, more frequent Demolish. Disc: Smite +40%, Flash Heal +25%.

    Trending down

    1. Mistweaver Monk. The only spec with a blanket -3% all healing, plus Spinning Crane Kick -15% and Way of the Crane 340%→280%. Mastery +50% only helps if you stack Mastery. Net down.
    2. Burst-identity specs (squish risk). Net-neutral by design, but if the compensation is under-tuned these come out behind: Elemental (Ascendance Overload -60%), Windwalker (burst -25/30%), Devourer (meta scaling -66%), Fire (Combustion halved), Ret (Art of War -47%, Divine Toll -50%).
    3. Fatebound Rogues. Deal Fate extra-combo-point chance 100%→60% (-40%) hits Assa & Outlaw Fatebound.
    4. Dark Ranger Hunters. Black Arrow direct damage -40% and Withering Fire’s Black Arrow -40%.
    5. Frost Mage Hero-talent burst. Hand of Frost R2 halved (1/2% → 0.5/1%).
    Build history: Frost DK and Enhancement Shaman were barely touched in build 1 but got full retunes in Wk2. Wk3 is a big raid-test tuning pass: broad nerfs to over-performers (Frost DK −10% all + Icy Death Torrent −35%, Unholy, Ret) and buffs to under-performers (Marksmanship, Holy Paladin and Holy Priest got their first PTR changes, Arms auto +100%). Only Demonology Warlock still has no spec-specific change across all three builds.

    Should you reroll?

    if it goes live as-is

    Short version: this is a redistribution patch on a first PTR build, so there is no free DPS reroll hiding in these notes and the numbers will change. Don’t reroll off one PTR build. By direction of travel:

    Want a DPS that genuinely got stronger, not reshuffled? Beast Mastery Hunter is the cleanest pick - buffed because it was weak, no nerf to its own burst.
    Healer main? Resto Druid is the trend-up healer. Disc/Holy/Pres are roughly neutral with QoL wins. Avoid rerolling to Mistweaver for output this build.
    Hate spiky, feast-or-famine gameplay? That is the whole point of the patch. Elemental, Windwalker, Devourer, Fire, Retribution will feel much flatter for similar totals. Reroll for feel, not numbers.
    Chasing big burst windows? Don’t reroll for this now - the on-use spikes are exactly what is being cut.
    AoE / Mythic+ focused? Elemental Shaman (Chain Lightning +60% + 5 targets, Flame Shock +60%) and Shadow Priest (new Shadeburst AoE) picked up dedicated multi-target damage.
    Reroll caveat: none of this is simmed. Net DPS depends on final coefficients, tier sets and encounter tuning this build does not lock in. Treat it as “which way the wind is blowing,” not a tier list.
