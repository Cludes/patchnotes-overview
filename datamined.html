2. Headline datamined findings

not yet in official notes

The biggest structural changes in build 68569 are design edits, not just number tweaks:

Datamined Evoker

Scalecommander Wingleader reworked

The hero talent that reduces Breath-spell cooldowns was heavily cut. Devastation’s Bombardments now reduce Deep Breath by 0.5 sec per target struck, capped at 1.5 sec; Preservation / Augmentation’s Mass Eruption reduces Breath of Eons by 1.0 sec per target, capped at 3.0 sec. Previously uncapped - a large nerf to Breath uptime.

Datamined Paladin

Protection talent tree reorganized

The Protection Paladin talent tree layout was rearranged in this build. A structural change to talent positioning rather than a raw damage number, so no single percentage - but it changes what builds are reachable.

Datamined All classes

This build touched many Season 2 class sets - values, redesigns, and a fully rebuilt Blood DK set. The confirmed designs are on the tier sets page; the datamined numeric tuning is in section 4 below.

3. Datamined spec-wide damage tuning

class aura old → new

Several specs have a single “Modifies Damage/Healing Done” class aura that scales all their damage. Build 68569 shifts these. The effective change is the ratio of the new multiplier to the old one - e.g. a +41% aura going to +46% is 1.46 / 1.41 = +3.5% to everything the aura touches. These are datamined and may not match the final official notes.

SpecClass-aura valueEffective all-damage change
Devourer Demon Hunter+10% → +21%+10%
Balance Druid+41% → +46%+3.5%
Havoc Demon Hunter+22% → +16%-4.9%
Unholy Death Knight-20% → -26%-7.5%
Frost Death Knight-2% → -12%-10.2%

Two single-ability buffs sit outside the table because they hit one spell, not the whole kit: Marksmanship Hunter’s Black Arrow (direct damage +20% → +50%, +25% effective; damage-over-time +50% → +87%, +24.7% effective), and Mistweaver Monk’s Spinning Crane Kick aura (+166% → +193%, +10.2% effective - a small change in practice, since Mistweaver is a healer and SCK is a minor part of its output).

5. Why datamined is not final

read before quoting these

Datamined values are a snapshot of a build in progress: internal test values, placeholders, and numbers that get re-tuned before release. Unholy Death Knight shows exactly how much a number can move - and it is a change Blizzard has already documented, so we can compare directly:

Unholy DK. Unholy is nerfed hard in Blizzard’s official Week 3 notes - a spec-wide “all damage” cut plus a stack of individual ability nerfs. Blizzard first posted that spec-wide cut at -8%, then hotfixed it to -3% after a bug had skewed the tuning. This datamined build shows its own spec-wide damage aura (the -20% → -26% line in section 3) and a new -25% Death Coil modifier - values that do not line up with that final -3%. Same direction (Unholy is getting nerfed), very different magnitude.

That is the whole point: even Blizzard’s official number moved five points via hotfix, so a raw datamined value is a work-in-progress reading, not final tuning. Rule of thumb: trust this page most where it agrees with the official notes (section 1), treat datamined-only numbers (sections 2-4) as strong signal, not gospel, and expect specifics to move before 12.1 ships.
